Jeff Pruitt says:
(Sat Oct 9 11:03:03 1999 169.132.153.113)
Hi. It's not a big deal, but I just got a couple of e-mails asking me about Sophia being quoted in PEOPLE about Sarah's weight and what that means. So I guess I'd better come on here and clear up any misunderstanding and give you guys the real story behind that:

PEOPLE called Sophia and told her they were doing a story on stuntwomen and how they keep fit to double famous actresses. She was supposed to be one of many others. She told them that she was 5' 3" tall (just like Sarah) and that the studio sent her to have her hair done by the same guy who did Sarah's hair. She told them that it was easy for her to match Sarah but that she had to be careful to not let herself get too muscular because Sarah (like most actresses) likes to keep really thin. They asked her some questions about her diet and training and then about Sarah's diet. She told them to ask Sarah. That was it.

Later she got a call from DATELINE. They wanted to fly her to N.Y. to appear on the show. By that time she had started to figure out that these people were not interested in stunts at all, but just wanted to get something they could make some gossip out of. So she turned them down flat.

Joss and I got a big laugh out of the quote from me in EW. In fact I recieved a hit to the back from him that I am sure must be some sort of "delayed death touch." I had mentioned to EW that Joss had told me to check out an old Danny Kaye movie in which Danny was barely able to defend himself with a sword and was quite inept at it. He toyed with the idea of making Buffy that way. It wasn't that he wanted to turn the final battle between Buffy & Angel into a COMEDY (as the quote made it seem). But that's how it goes. He told me that things he says are taken out of context and misunderstood all the time.

I can't imagine what it must be like for the actors. Sometimes they must wonder who those people are writing about. Things WILL always be twisted around to mean whatever the author thinks sounds best to sell the article. That's usually the way these things go.

btw - It wasn't a "stunt gone awry" as mentioned in the EW article. It was a trap set by "special fx" - not stunts. Sophia was simply on the dangerous end of the unspecial effect that night.

thanks,

- Jeff Pruitt
