Jane Espenson (Writer) says:
(Sat Nov 20 22:18:06 1999 152.163.195.204)

Advice for getting started. First step: be young. In general, TV writing is a career for which one has to start out young. It's terrible but true.
Then, read as many professional TV scripts as you can. -- there are several ways to get these... some LA bookstores have them, XFiles scripts can be purchased through the fan club, etc. Also read books on how to write for TV. I began with a book called "How to write for TV" By Madeline DiMaggio.
Then, write spec scripts (= sample scripts) for the very best shows on TV.
You may also want to get some instruction. After I read Madeline DiMaggio's book, I signed up for a class she taught in San Francisco.
Then, get a list of agents from the Writers' Guild of America. (You can get their # from information in L.A.).
Send your scripts to the agents that accept unsolicited scripts. If they're good, you might just get an agent!
OR... there's a shortcut... an open door that not enough people know about. Star Trek shows (TNG in my day, Voyager now), accept scripts written FOR THEIR SHOW, without an agent!!!! It's a completely open door! This is how I got my first TV sale, which lead to my first agent... etc.

Hopethis helps! I also recommend new writers to enter every contest and fellowship competition they can find. After my initial Trek success I got accepted into the Disney Writer's Fellowship, which got me first sitcom job (on the Disney show Dinosaurs.)
