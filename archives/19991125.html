Acid Cross - Don't do like I did (to quote Dr. Dre) At least wear pants. My forgetting to cloth the lower half has cost me many job opportunities.

Sita - Everybody goes through a different door. It's sorta like the Circus. You have to get training and hook up with the right people. Everyone has a background in something (gymnastics, Martial arts, motocross, cars, high diving) Then we learn to put the physical skills to use in a different way by adding the stunt skills to our other skills. Then a coordinator has to take a chance on hiring you after you've gotten the SAG card. It usually takes a some years to do all of this. But then there is no assurance of continued employment. It's a crap shoot. There are less than 1700 pro stunt people and only 200 of us make a good living at it. That lucky 200 takes almost all the jobs. That's why we all know of each other. It's a tiny town.
