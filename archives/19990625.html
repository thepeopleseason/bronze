J.S.K. Thanks for the tip on the "Ooh La La" TV show. That one sounds cool too. This is good.

Cosmic Bob: I guarantee the CD will be worth the wait. Guaranteed. Of course I'm about as biased as you can get, but let's put it this way... I'm real happy with what's happening in the studio right now.

Mesuvius: Yes, most of the songs are written. Actually, we have enough songs for about 3 albums right now. In the mean time, new songs come out of nowhere and find themselves in contention. I'm recording a lot of stuff and developing new things at the same time. What ends up on this particular record will depend on a lot of things including how the songs fit together. Sometimes you can have a great song that just does not seem to want to fit in with the others. So, you save it for later. A great record is like a 12 course meal. It has to work individually and as a whole. I really didn't have a chance to do this on the Warm CD because we were really hurried. To be honest, the Warm CD consists of the first 10 tracks I was able to produce that didn't have something that really bugged me (plus 3 remixes). Figuring out the tune stack on that record was a real challenge, let me tell you.

Shadowslayer: I believe you are thinking of a Video contest thing going on with MTV. We sent in a video of the song "Emily."

Lou Who: I figure Sarah probably doesn't care much if I spell her name wrong. She's not the type.

AngelLover24-7: Well... nothing in the music business is entirely based on talent. A lot of it is based on politics, who you know, and simpley being in the right place at the right time (luck). I mean, it was total luck when Velvet Chain got on Buffy. Somebody on the staff saw a gig and brought in the CD and boom, they called us. At this time, Buffy was just starting out, so we were like, "ok, what's this 'Buffy' thing? Do we really want to be on this show? What if it's stupid? Well, we need the exposure, so what the hell." In the mean time, the show turns out to be one of the coolest shows on television, and we end up with a ton of new fans. All because 1 guy from the production staff happend to be at one of our shows. It's crazy. I must say, however, if the band was not very good, we would never have made it onto the show because the people who produce Buffy definitely know good from not good (which is more than I can say for a lot of the music industry). Remember the band "Presidents of the United States?" What the hell was that?

Kenickie: Our gig is actually tomorrow night, not tonight.

PeaceFire: Thank you so much for the "vote for Velvet Chain for Lilith Fair" plugs you are putting up on the board. You totally rock. May I make a suggestion, however. The voting site is run by xoom.com, which is a lame site. It's super hard to find Velvet Chain or any other band, once you get there. I recommend that anybody who wants to vote, go the the Velvet Chain site at www.velvetchain.com and pick up the link there. We update the link daily so that you end up on the exact page Velvet Chain is on, so you don't have to bang around looking for us (pretty smart eh?) And thank you again.
