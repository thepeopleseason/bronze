NancyE - I don't read scripts. They have offered to send them to me but they don't do me much good - I can get a much better idea of the pacing, performances, and look & feel of a show from a rough cut. They send me a rough cut usually one or two days before the final cut. This is when I get to see the eps for the first time. Sometimes shots are missing, and (of course) music and SFX are temporary if they're there at all, but it gives me an idea. Then, once there's a final cut, I sit down with Joss & co. and we watch the whole show, deciding specifically where music starts and ends and what it should do. Thisis called "spotting". Now comes the fun part: I wait until the last possible moment, doing everything BUT what I should be doing: writing the score. Then I do a first draft of the score, Joss gives me notes, I make my revisions, and poof! I'm done. Typically most of the first draft (like, 95%) ends up in the final.
