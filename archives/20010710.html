back in the fall of 1997, i harbored a dirty little secret: i was addicted to this show called (of all the humiliating things) "Buffy the Vampire Slayer". i struggled valiantly to repress my deep-seated need to analyze, adore, and deconstruct every episode in its minutiae, but alas, i was just as hooked as your average crack ho. luckily, my dealer had this on-line shop where i could (for the relatively small price of eye-strain and carpal-tunnel) indulge my soul-quaking lust with impunity -- moreover, i could bond with other addicts across the globe. and now...

well, you know the story. i've gone from passive crack ho to dealing the stuff myself -- a journey that has had its ups and downs, its twists and turns, but ultimately was so rewarding and worthy that the aches and pains seem to POOF right into dustage. i'll always remember The Bronze as my starting place, my own personal Genesis [sans Phil Collins], the Big Bang that created my universe as i know it today, and to even contemplate that it could've happened otherwise is to stare into the Hellmouth itself.

i know there will be another Bronze, somewhere, sometime -- and yet, my heart belongs to the original. i will always consider this Bronze to be my first home, and though i might move away, dye my hair a freaky shade of purple, join a commune, get a job as an investment banker, or any of the other wacky things people do when they grow up, i know that i've come from a good place. a loving, supportive place. and i will always be thankful.

i was, am, and always will be...

a Bronzer.
