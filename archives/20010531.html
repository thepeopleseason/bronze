joss says:
(Thu May 31 20:11:19 2001 64.12.102.164)
Now EVERYBODY'S posting about Fray! Oh, I love you ALL! Group cyber-hug! (It's just scary now. He's like a monster.) So, Fray is a comic about a slayer a few hundred years from now that is the first comic I ever wrote ever and I'm wicked excited about it. I THINK it can be bought at Darkhorse.com, they're the publishers.

The thing is, it's hard to find comics ANYWHERE outside the major urban areas. Everyone says Comics are a dying industry. But I want people to be able to find Fray, and this goes way beyond my shameless plugging. I want people to feel what I did when I read comics as a kid. I ran to the newstand every day (I didn't realiae they came out once a week, I went every day till they showed, I wasn't bright in the traditional sense) to find the latest issues of my favorite books, and I want kids to do that again. But (old man speech coming) when I grew up we didn't even have home video, let alone what we have now. A movie came out, you saw it as many times as you could in the theatre before it left 'cause you would never see it again. The only true epic you could bring home with you was a comic book. Maybe they aren't needed anymore, but they're SUCH an extraordinary medium. So even if it isn't FRAY, if people hate it, I want that medium to live on. Superman. Batman. Planetary. Whatever. Comics shouldn't die.

(single tear rolls down his cheek. Also some mucus visible.)

Yes, I'm working on Angel with newcomer Brett Matthews. We plot it out together, he writes the scripts and I edit and polish. We're like a superhero team, except we don't have powers or costumes and we're pretty much okay with the idea of evil.

The idea was to make the comic more comicky -- it felt like they were keeping too close to the show (my fault, by the way) so we decided to give him a rogues gallery and some over the top-cow action and sexiness. (hopefully not gratuitous). We're doing a four issue arc and laying the seeds for further arcs. It's big fun.
