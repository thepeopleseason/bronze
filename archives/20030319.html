Nikka and Sarcastic Kelly (luv yer name!)

You're actually both right in a way. I'm supposed to check for continuity within the script. For example if Buffy refers to a conversation that happened in a Season 3, I would check to make sure that the 1) the conversation did infact take place and 2) the the content of the reference is correct.

I also look for things like is a character appears in the beginning of a scene, but then is missing in a later continuation of the scene.

The script supervisor checks the continuity on set during the actual filming. (i.e. which arm someone has a cast on, if the were eating or drinking, was the pony tail over the right or left shoulder.)

The writer's are very conscious of trying to maintain the continuity of the the entire Buffy story because they fully realize that it is important to the fans of the show. But sometimes, things slip through the cracks.
