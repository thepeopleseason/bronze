Angle Man - Me? Procrastinate? Actually I'm off for a while, no Buffy till sometime next week. Just finished ep 17. Anyone want a spoiler?

Wolfguard - good questions. While writing the show, the music editor (Fernand Bos) gives the music cues simple, denotative titles, as well as a numeric identifier, which help me to keep track of them all. I'm making these up here (I don't have access to the original notes) but the Buffy/Angel cue in GD2 could have been called "Angel Leaves", the dream sequence "Buffy's Dream". They also get the numeric identifiers like 1m5 which means the fifth cue in Act 1. For use in public I try to come up with more interesting titles (on one CD I made, I called the dream sequence "Counting Down from 730"). Christopher Young (excellent film composer) always has very clever titles on his soundtracks.

The shift in tone in GD2 as Buffy looks at Faith's unconcious body was deliberate and requested specifically by Joss to be at that moment. He really wanted the audience to feel Buffy's determination coming to her in a rush as she was looking at Faith - a definite transition from the affection she's feeling for Faith to a fierce resolve to kill the mayor. Musically it's a shift from slow, high strings and woodwind, to medium tempo, insistent low brass. This is a fantastic example of the power of music - you look at it without any sound, and it's just Buffy staring. But with the music, you really feel the shift in her mindset.
