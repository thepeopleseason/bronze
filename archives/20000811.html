Hello.

I've been too busy to even answer email for the past three weeks, but someone asked me to come on and answer a question (same one again) so I will. He can read it here later.

Hope you don't mind.

The answer is this: Yes, I did choreograph the fights and Sophia performed them - every one of them. (And every fall, burn, car hit and everything else physical that Buffy did.)

As far as Sarah being a Martial artist goes: I'll have to say the same thing that I have always said. It doesn't have anything to do with acting, nor should it matter to anyone's enjoyment of the show. It's just that if I answer any other way I would be just another Hollywood phony. That I cannot do. I have to stand up for the contributions of stunt people. I'm one of the original people involved in getting the Emmy Awards to begin to look into creating a new stunt award. We are still working on it. My profession has a lot at stake in this kind of info being related openly and honestly.

Sarah has NEVER done a gymnastics move in her life. She can't even kip-up. She is NOT a Martial artist. Not by a long shot. I know. I've been involved in Martial arts for over 28 years. I've been doing fights on film for 18 of those years. And I am the guy who made those "Buffy" fights you've been watching on TV. For those reasons there is no person more qualified to answer this question than myself. Please accept that. Because it is the truth.

So far this 5th season there are two stunt doubles doing most of the physical work. That's just how it is. Sarah is a great actress, but not a Martial artist or gymnast. That's all. The stunt people deserve the credit for that part of the show.

So that is the answer to that question. Despite whatever new articles or phony bio info or PR you may find coming out.

If you want to see a real article on BUFFY, I suggest checking out next month's SFX magazine. Lots of color pics with Sophia , me and an Aly interview with pics too. A good one.

Okay. Hope that clears that up. I'm working with the executive board that we've set up with the Academy towards promoting honesty in our biz so that our stunt people are not prevented from getting the rewards that they deserve for their work. It's not easy to do. And it doesn't always go down well with those who would rather keep you in the dark about what we do. We have to be open about it. We have to try. Otherwise things will never change for those who come after us.

Hope you guys will understand and will pass this answer around to any who bring up the question again.

Thanks very much,

Jeff Pruitt
